Introduction

To The City

Bhaktapur, also known as Khwopa is a city in the west corner of the Kathmandu Valley in Nepal about 8 miles or 13 kilometers from the capital city of Nepal, Kathmandu. It is located in and serves as the headquater of Bhaktapur District in Bagmati Pradesh of Nepal. It is administratively divided into 10 wards..

Of Bhaktapur

It is the home of traditional art and architecture, historical monuments and craft work, magnificent windows, pottery and weaving industries, excellent temples, beautiful ponds, rich local customs, culture, religion, festivals, musical mystic and so on. Bhaktapur is still an untouched as well as preserved ancient city that is itself the world to explore for tourists. Here are so many heritage sites to view Nepal's ancient history. Landmarks

To Visit

Bhaktapur Durbar Square is a conglomeration of pagoda and shikhara style temples, mostly dedicated to Hindu gods and goddesses grouped around a 55-window palace of brick and wood. The square is one of the most charming architectural showpieces of the valley as it highlights the ancient arts of Nepal.
